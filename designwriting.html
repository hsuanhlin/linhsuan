The Birth of Neutrality

Tracing back the birth of newspapers, it dates as early as 202 b.c. in China called 'tipao' during the Han dynasty; 59 b.c. when Julius Caesar decreed that political happenings, trials and executions should be posted publicly – in piles of hand-written sheets/carved in metal or stone. Widespread of automation and invention of innovated-movable type printing press by Johannes Gutenberg; Linotype machine by Ottmar Mergenthaler, accelerate the mass production of documents (pamphlets/news sheets/news books/news ballads). The revolution in method of knowledge stretched out across Asia then Europe by 1500. The first weekly newspaper was published in Germany by Johann Carolus in 1604, and the first American newspaper was published on the thanksgiving in 1690. Newspaper is the symbol of building trust, authenticity and originality within the public through politics (initially). Its full scope of topics include the construction of news as a cultural practice.

10-mile long roll of newsprint paper turn into 30,000 copies of the New York Times in 5 hours. From digital text to tangible silver plates which are made in the room with special yellow lighting that helps protect the plates muted images from exposure. Wrapped in plastic, then delivered with clamp truck directly to newspaper stands and facade of residency place. 12 inches wide by 22 inches long for a folded pages, large commercial dailies expanded six to eight pages with eight to ten columns of text, it maintained its standard since nineteenth century but with a slightly change in width (originally 13.5 in). “I use pencil and paper to sketch out the page because it is the fastest, most fluid way for me to visualize the interrelation of the various elements and work out an arrangement that will best express the news and our newspaper for that day,” stated Tom Bodkin, the design director of the New York Times. He is interested in their modulation, rhythm and balance, using a schematic is one of the most efficient way for him to sketched out ‘multilayered puzzle.’

Newspaper is beyond a ‘paper of record’ contemporarily. The quantity of newspapers in the US hits its peak in 1909 and it’s declined ever since. The way how news spread transformed into medias within technology, however, timing is always a vital factor in the industry. The purpose of news is to report about the past, the present and the future, to convey somebody’s opinion concerning a contemporary event. News must be neutral and unprejudiced as much as possible. It should be based on true facts, participants’ and experts’ opinions, but not on a journalist’s point of view.
