Behavior
"The way in which someone conducts oneself or behaves; the manner of conducting, or the way in which something functions or operates" (Merriam-Webster).
I reference behavior as evidence of the reciprocal relationship or collision between human elements.


Boundary
"Something that indicates or fixes a limit or extent" (Merriam-Webster).
I reference boundaries as the dichotomy of human elements. More specifically, this dichotomy refers to cultural, social, and political disparities in society.


Causality
"The relationship between cause and effect; the principle that everything has a cause" (Oxford Dictionary).
I reference casualty as an uncertain and unexpected transitional framework in the present that instantly combines temporal and spatial elements.


Interplay
"The effect that two or more things have on each other; the action between two or more things or the effect they have on each other" (Cambridge Dictionary).
I reference interplay as a reciprocal relationship or collision between elements within certain embedded human systems.


Mundane
"Of, relating to, or characteristic of the world"(Merriam Webster). "Very ordinary and therefore not interesting" (Cambridge Dictionary).
I reference the mundane as repetitive, structured phenomena, but also the foundation of everything. It is "the key to seeing things in a new way" (Harry West, the principal of Invisible Design).


Perception
"A belief or opinion, often held by many people and based on how things seem, or the quality of being aware of things through the physical senses, especially sight" (Cambridge Dictionary).
I reference perception as the interplay and individuality of worldviews between and amongst human particles.


Sociology
"The science of society, social institutions, and social relationships. Specifically: the systematic study of the development, structure, interaction, and collective behavior of organized groups of human beings; the scientific analysis of a social institution as a functioning whole and as it relates to the rest of society” (Merriam-Webster).
I reference sociology as the collective multiplicity of humanizing traits and characteristics that constitute society.


System
"A regularly interacting or interdependent group of items forming a unified whole a number system; an organized set of doctrines, ideas, or principles usually intended to explain the arrangement or working of a systematic whole the Newtonian system of mechanics" (Merriam-Webster).
I refer systems as algorithmic solutions that support the evidence of visual patterns, which are formed by organized and interdependent societal principles.


Visual Activism
I refer to a visual activism as the proactive trigger and effect of the relationship between activists practices and contemporary visual culture.
