Behavior
"The way in which someone conducts oneself or behaves, the manner of conducting, or the way in which something functions or operates." (Merriam-Webster)
I refer to a behavior as the evidence of reciprocal relationship or collision between elements.


Boundary
"Something that indicates or fixes a limit or extent." (Merriam-Webster)
I refer to a boundary as the surface of collision, more specifically, an usual cultural, social, and political limitation in structure.


Causality
"The relationship between cause and effect, or the principle that everything has a cause." (Oxford Dictionary)
I refer to a casualty as uncertain and unexpected transitional timeframe in the present that instantly combines temporality and spatiality.


Interplay
"The effect that two or more things have on each other, or the action between two or more things or the effect they have on each other." (Cambridge Dictionary)
I refer to an interplay as a reciprocal relationship or collision between elements with certain embedded strategies.


Mundane
"Of, relating to, or characteristic of the world." (Merriam Webster) "Very ordinary and therefore not interesting." (Cambridge Dictionary)
I refer to a mundane as "the key to seeing things in a new way." (Harry West, the principal of Invisible Design) It is repetitive, structured but also the foundation of everything.


Perception
"A belief or opinion, often held by many people and based on how things seem, or the quality of being aware of things through the physical senses, especially sight." (Cambridge Dictionary)


Sociology
"The science of society, social institutions, and social relationships specifically. The systematic study of the development, structure, interaction, and collective behavior of organized groups of human beings. The scientific analysis of a social institution as a functioning whole and as it relates to the rest of society." (Merriam-Webster)


System
"A regularly interacting or interdependent group of items forming a unified whole a number system, or an organized set of doctrines, ideas, or principles usually intended to explain the arrangement or working of a systematic whole the Newtonian system of mechanics." (Merriam-Webster)
I refer to a system as an algorithmic solution that supports the evidence of visual patterns, which is formed by organized and interdependent principles.


Visual Activism
"Art Activism or Artivism utilizes the medium of visual art as a method of social or political commentary." (Merriam-Webster)
I refer to a visual activism as the effect of relationship between activists practices and contemporary visual culture. It is a visually proactive trigger.
